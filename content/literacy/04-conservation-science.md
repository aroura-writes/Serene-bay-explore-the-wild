## The Sixth Mass Extinction

Earth has experienced five mass extinctions in its 4.5 billion year history — catastrophic events that eliminated the majority of species alive at the time. Scientists now warn that we are in the midst of a sixth mass extinction, this time driven entirely by a single species: Homo sapiens.

Current extinction rates are estimated to be 100 to 1,000 times higher than natural background rates. The International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) currently lists over 42,100 species as threatened with extinction — including 41% of all amphibians, 26% of mammals, and 14% of birds.

## The Power of Rewilding

Rewilding is one of conservation science's most exciting frontiers: the large-scale restoration of ecosystems to the point where nature can take care of itself. Unlike traditional conservation's focus on protecting what remains, rewilding actively restores what has been lost.

The reintroduction of wolves to Yellowstone National Park in 1995 produced a phenomenon called a "trophic cascade." Wolves controlled deer populations, which allowed riverside vegetation to recover, which stabilized river banks, which changed the course of rivers — literally altering the physical geography of the park. This demonstrates that nature, when given the chance, has a power and intelligence we are only beginning to understand.

## Field Facts

- Protecting 30% of land and oceans could halt 70% of projected species extinctions
- Every $1 invested in nature restoration returns $30 in ecosystem benefits
- Indigenous-managed lands contain 80% of the world's remaining biodiversity
