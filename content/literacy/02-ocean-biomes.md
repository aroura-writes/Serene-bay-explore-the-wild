## The Ocean as a Climate Engine

The ocean covers 71% of Earth's surface and contains 97% of the planet's water. But its significance goes far beyond its size. The ocean acts as a massive climate regulator, absorbing more than 90% of the excess heat caused by climate change and approximately 25–30% of all human CO₂ emissions.

The ocean drives global weather patterns through thermohaline circulation — a vast system of ocean currents driven by differences in water temperature and salinity. This "global conveyor belt" distributes heat across the planet, making temperate climates in northern Europe possible and regulating monsoon patterns across Asia.

## Coral Reefs: The Rainforests of the Sea

Despite covering less than 0.1% of the ocean floor, coral reefs support approximately 25% of all known marine species. They are among the most biodiverse ecosystems on Earth — rivaled only by tropical rainforests in the sheer density of life they sustain.

Coral reefs are not the simple, passive structures they appear. Each coral polyp is an animal — a tiny predator that feeds on microscopic plankton at night. The vivid colors of coral reefs come from symbiotic algae called zooxanthellae living within the coral tissue, providing up to 90% of the coral's energy through photosynthesis.

## Field Facts

- The ocean produces over 50% of Earth's oxygen
- Less than 20% of Earth's oceans have been mapped in high resolution
- The deep ocean is the largest habitat on Earth by volume
